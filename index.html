// 01 — common attacks

Know Your Enemy

🎣
PHISHING DANGER LEVEL: ████████░░ HIGH

Phishing is when attackers pretend to be a trusted source — like your bank, Google, or even your school — to trick you into giving away passwords, credit card numbers, or personal information.

How it works:

  • Hacker sends a fake email pretending to be your bank
  • You click a link that looks real but leads to a fake site
  • You enter your username and password
  • Hacker now has your login credentials
🕵️
MAN-IN-THE-MIDDLE DANGER LEVEL: ███████░░░ HIGH

In a Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) attack, a hacker secretly intercepts communication between two parties — like you and your bank — without either side knowing. They can read, steal, or even modify the data.

How it works:

  • You connect to public Wi-Fi at a café
  • Attacker intercepts your connection silently
  • All your data passes through the attacker's device
  • Attacker reads your passwords, messages, and data
💥
DDoS ATTACK DANGER LEVEL: ██████████ CRITICAL

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack floods a website or server with millions of fake requests from thousands of hacked computers — causing it to crash and become unavailable to real users.

How it works:

  • Hacker infects thousands of computers (botnet)
  • All infected devices attack one target simultaneously
  • Target server gets overwhelmed with fake traffic
  • Website crashes — real users cannot access it
🦠
RANSOMWARE DANGER LEVEL: ██████████ CRITICAL

Ransomware is malicious software that encrypts all your files and demands payment (ransom) to unlock them. It has attacked hospitals, schools, and governments worldwide.

How it works:

  • You download a malicious email attachment
  • Ransomware silently encrypts all your files
  • A message demands payment (Bitcoin) to decrypt
  • Even paying doesn't guarantee file recovery
🪄
SOCIAL ENGINEERING DANGER LEVEL: █████████░ CRITICAL

Social engineering manipulates people psychologically rather than hacking computers. Attackers pretend to be IT support, bosses, or friends to trick you into giving access.

How it works:

  • Attacker calls pretending to be IT support
  • They say your computer is infected and need access
  • You give them remote control of your device
  • They steal data or install malware
💉
SQL INJECTION DANGER LEVEL: ████████░░ HIGH

SQL Injection tricks a website's database into revealing secret data. Hackers type special code into login forms to bypass passwords and steal entire databases.

How it works:

  • Hacker types SQL code in the login box
  • Poorly coded website treats it as a command
  • Database leaks usernames, passwords, and data
  • Hacker gains full access without a password
🧲
KEYLOGGER DANGER LEVEL: ███████░░░ HIGH

A keylogger is hidden software (or hardware) that records every key you press — capturing passwords, credit card numbers, and messages without you knowing.

How it works:

  • Malware installs secretly via download or USB
  • Every keystroke you type is recorded silently
  • Logs are sent to the attacker automatically
  • Attacker extracts your passwords and data
📱
SMISHING / VISHING DANGER LEVEL: ████████░░ HIGH

Smishing is phishing via SMS text messages. Vishing is phishing via phone calls. Attackers send fake texts or call pretending to be your bank or government.

How it works:

  • You receive a text: "Your package is stuck — click here"
  • Link leads to a fake site asking for your details
  • Or a caller claims to be your bank's fraud team
  • They collect your OTP, PIN, or card number
🕳️
ZERO-DAY EXPLOIT DANGER LEVEL: ██████████ CRITICAL

A zero-day exploit targets unknown security flaws in software that even the developer hasn't discovered yet — so there's no patch available. These are the most dangerous attacks.

How it works:

  • Hacker discovers an unknown flaw in software
  • They exploit it before the company knows it exists
  • No antivirus or patch can stop it
  • Sold on dark web for millions of dollars

// 04 — protection

Stay Safe Online

🔐

STRONG PASSWORDS

Use long, complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across sites.

Minimum 12 characters
Mix uppercase & lowercase
Include numbers & symbols
Use a password manager
🔍

SPOT PHISHING EMAILS

Always check the sender's email address. Real companies never ask for passwords via email.

Check sender email carefully
Don't click suspicious links
Look for HTTPS in browser
Call company directly to verify
🛡️

USE A VPN

A VPN encrypts all your internet traffic, protecting you from MITM attacks on public Wi-Fi networks.

Always use VPN on public Wi-Fi
Check for HTTPS websites
Avoid banking on public networks
Use trusted VPN providers only
🔄

KEEP SOFTWARE UPDATED

Updates patch security vulnerabilities. Old software is easy to hack. Always keep OS and apps updated.

Enable automatic updates
Update antivirus regularly
Remove unused apps
Update router firmware
📱

TWO-FACTOR AUTH (2FA)

Even if a hacker gets your password, 2FA stops them. Always enable it on important accounts.

Enable 2FA on all accounts
Use authenticator apps
Never share OTP codes
Use hardware keys for banks
💾

BACKUP YOUR DATA

Regular backups protect you from ransomware attacks. If your data is held hostage, you can restore it.

Backup weekly to external drive
Use cloud backups (Google Drive)
Test backups regularly
Keep 3 copies of important data
// 05 — legal awareness

Cyber Laws in Nepal

Nepal's primary cyber law is the Electronic Transaction Act (ETA), 2063 (2008). It defines and criminalizes cybercrimes and is enforced by the Cyber Bureau of Nepal Police.

साइबर अपराध • ELECTRONIC TRANSACTION ACT 2063

SECTION 44
ETA 2063 — PIRACY / SOURCE CODE

Pirating, destroying, or altering legally protected computer source code with malicious intent.

⚖ Penalty: Up to 3 years imprisonment + fine up to NPR 2,000

SECTION 45
ETA 2063 — UNAUTHORIZED ACCESS / HACKING

Accessing any computer, program, or data without proper authorization from the owner — i.e. hacking.

⚖ Penalty: Up to 3 years imprisonment + fine up to NPR 2,00,000

SECTION 46
ETA 2063 — DAMAGE TO COMPUTER SYSTEMS

Intentionally destroying, deleting, altering, or disrupting any computer data or system to cause wrongful loss (e.g. ransomware, DDoS).

⚖ Penalty: Up to 3 years imprisonment + fine up to NPR 2,000

SECTION 47
ETA 2063 — ILLEGAL ONLINE CONTENT

Publishing obscene, immoral, hateful, or socially harmful content on the internet, computer, or any electronic media. Most commonly used section in Nepal.

⚖ Penalty: Up to 5 years imprisonment + fine up to NPR 1,00,000

SECTION 48
ETA 2063 — CONFIDENTIALITY BREACH

Revealing or leaking confidential records, documents, or information accessed through authority of this Act without lawful authorization.

⚖ Penalty: Up to 2 years imprisonment + fine up to NPR 10,000

SECTION 51
ETA 2063 — COMPUTER FRAUD / PHISHING

Committing fraud using digital means — fake digital signatures, ATM card fraud, eSewa/Khalti fraud, phishing scams, and online financial deception.

⚖ Penalty: Up to 2 years imprisonment + fine up to NPR 1,00,000

⚖ SUPPORTING LAWS USED ALONGSIDE ETA 2063
MULUKI CRIMINAL CODE 2074 Sections 294–307 cover cyberbullying, harassment, defamation, identity theft, and privacy violations committed online.
INDIVIDUAL PRIVACY ACT 2075 (2018) Protects personal data and privacy. Covers data breaches, sextortion, and unauthorized sharing of private information.
BANKING OFFENCE ACT 2064 Covers online banking fraud, eSewa/Khalti/IMEPay phishing, ATM skimming, and financial cyber fraud.
DRAFT IT BILL (PENDING) A new Information Technology Bill is under parliamentary review (2026) — proposes stricter data protection, platform registration, and a Cyber Security Authority.

Careers in Cybersecurity

The world needs 3.5 million more cybersecurity professionals by 2025. This could be YOUR career.

🕵️
ETHICAL HACKER

Legally hack systems to find vulnerabilities before real hackers do. Also called "Penetration Tester".

AVG SALARY: ₹8–25 LPA
🔬
MALWARE ANALYST

Reverse-engineer viruses and malware to understand how they work and build defenses against them.

AVG SALARY: ₹10–30 LPA
🛡️
SOC ANALYST

Monitor a company's network 24/7 in a Security Operations Center. First to respond to live cyberattacks.

AVG SALARY: ₹6–20 LPA
🔐
CRYPTOGRAPHER

Design encryption algorithms that protect data. Work on securing banking, military, and government communications.

AVG SALARY: ₹15–40 LPA
🌐
NETWORK SECURITY ENG.

Design and protect entire company networks. Set up firewalls, VPNs, and intrusion detection systems.

AVG SALARY: ₹8–22 LPA

CISO

Chief Information Security Officer — the top cyber leader of a company. Oversees all security strategy and teams.

AVG SALARY: ₹50–2 Crore LPA
