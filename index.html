Know Your Enemy

🎣
PHISHING DANGER LEVEL: ████████░░ HIGH

Phishing is when attackers pretend to be a trusted source — like your bank, Google, or even your school — to trick you into giving away passwords, credit card numbers, or personal information.

How it works:

  • Hacker sends a fake email pretending to be your bank
  • You click a link that looks real but leads to a fake site
  • You enter your username and password
  • Hacker now has your login credentials
🕵️
MAN-IN-THE-MIDDLE DANGER LEVEL: ███████░░░ HIGH

In a Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) attack, a hacker secretly intercepts communication between two parties — like you and your bank — without either side knowing. They can read, steal, or even modify the data.

How it works:

  • You connect to public Wi-Fi at a café
  • Attacker intercepts your connection silently
  • All your data passes through the attacker's device
  • Attacker reads your passwords, messages, and data
💥
DDoS ATTACK DANGER LEVEL: ██████████ CRITICAL

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack floods a website or server with millions of fake requests from thousands of hacked computers — causing it to crash and become unavailable to real users.

How it works:

  • Hacker infects thousands of computers (botnet)
  • All infected devices attack one target simultaneously
  • Target server gets overwhelmed with fake traffic
  • Website crashes — real users cannot access it

// 03 — protection

Stay Safe Online

🔐

STRONG PASSWORDS

Use long, complex passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across sites.

Minimum 12 characters
Mix uppercase & lowercase
Include numbers & symbols
Use a password manager
🔍

SPOT PHISHING EMAILS

Always check the sender's email address. Real companies never ask for passwords via email.

Check sender email carefully
Don't click suspicious links
Look for HTTPS in browser
Call company directly to verify
🛡️

USE A VPN

A VPN encrypts all your internet traffic, protecting you from MITM attacks on public Wi-Fi networks.

Always use VPN on public Wi-Fi
Check for HTTPS websites
Avoid banking on public networks
Use trusted VPN providers only
🔄

KEEP SOFTWARE UPDATED

Updates patch security vulnerabilities. Old software is easy to hack. Always keep OS and apps updated.

Enable automatic updates
Update antivirus regularly
Remove unused apps
Update router firmware
📱

TWO-FACTOR AUTH (2FA)

Even if a hacker gets your password, 2FA stops them. Always enable it on important accounts.

Enable 2FA on all accounts
Use authenticator apps
Never share OTP codes
Use hardware keys for banks
💾

BACKUP YOUR DATA

Regular backups protect you from ransomware attacks. If your data is held hostage, you can restore it.

Backup weekly to external drive
Use cloud backups (Google Drive)
Test backups regularly
Keep 3 copies of important data
